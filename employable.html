Employable Resume Builder Prototype

Introduction

In today’s competitive job market, a standout resume can make all the difference. Recognizing this need, I set out to create Employable, a mobile application designed to simplify the resume-building process for job seekers. This case study outlines the design process, target audience, color palette, goals, objectives, and challenges encountered along the way.

Design Process

The journey began with extensive research into existing resume builders to identify common pain points and areas for improvement. I conducted user interviews and surveys to gather insights on what users found frustrating about current applications. This feedback was instrumental in shaping the features of Employable.

User Persona

Target Audience

  • Young professionals looking to create a resume quickly and easily.
  • Recent graduates who need a simple, intuitive tool to build their resumes.
  • Tech-savvy individuals who prefer mobile applications for on-the-go tasks.
  • Job seekers in various industries requiring customizable templates.
  • Users who value simplicity, flexibility, and ease of use in digital tools.

Color Palette

  • Primary color: #9B57D8 (Purple)
  • Secondary color: #F1D26D (Yellow)
  • Accent color: #FFB6A1 (Peachy pink)
  • Background color: #FFFFFF (White)

Goals and Objectives

The primary goals of Employable are: To provide an intuitive platform for users to create professional resumes quickly. To offer customizable templates that cater to various industries. To ensure mobile compatibility for users who prefer to build resumes on their smartphones.

Features

  • User-friendly interface that simplifies resume building for all skill levels.
  • Customizable templates tailored to various industries and job types.
  • Real-time preview of the resume as it’s being created.
  • Ability to export the resume as a PDF or share directly via email.
  • Step-by-step guidance through the resume creation process.
  • Integration with LinkedIn to easily import work history and skills.

Shortcomings

  • The mobile app is not yet available for Android devices (only iOS).
  • Limited template customization options (future updates will address this).
  • Some users report that certain resume sections are unclear for beginners.
  • Feedback from initial testers indicated that the onboarding process could be smoother.
  • While the app is free, premium features require a subscription, which may deter some users.

Conclusion

Overall, the Employable app aims to help job seekers create polished, professional resumes with ease. While there are a few shortcomings, the app’s user-friendly design and ability to simplify the resume-building process make it an invaluable tool for young professionals and graduates looking to enter the job market. Moving forward, the focus will be on expanding platform support, enhancing template customization, and improving the onboarding experience.
